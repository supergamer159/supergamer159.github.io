01

Core Game Concept

A brutal reliquary reclaimed one room at a time

Title Ashen Reliquary
Genre Top-down room-based action roguelite
Subgenre Trap-tactics dungeon crawler
Target Single-player PC

High Concept

The Last Ember Priory sends living vessels into a desecrated underground cathedral known as the Reliquary. Every chamber seals shut on entry. To survive, the player must read the room, defeat its wave composition, and convert the chamber's fixtures into temporary holy weapons before moving deeper.

The fantasy is not just "fight through a dungeon." It is "win by mastering the dungeon itself." The most powerful moments come from baiting a skeleton captain into a consecrated flamethrower lane, pulling a vampire through spikes, or turning a row of wall torches into a burn-heavy control zone.

Design Pillars

  • Every room is readable in under two seconds.
  • The environment is part of the combat kit, not background art.
  • Build diversity comes from systems interacting, not a huge asset burden.
  • Strict asset ceilings push remixing, variation, and polish over content sprawl.

Target Audience

Players who love aggressive action roguelites but also want positional play, room planning, and clean combat reads.

Session Goal

45 to 70 seconds per standard room, 2 to 4 minutes per boss, and a full victory run in 30 to 40 minutes.

Market Position

Premium indie action game with the replay loop of a roguelite and the authored clarity of a tactical dungeon brawler.

Dungeon Tileset

All architecture, room topology, and collision language comes from one 16x16 gothic tile source.

Two Vessels

The two player character sheets become two playable vessels with different starting loadouts and pacing.

Priests as Anchors

Three priest sprite families define the sanctuary, blessings, healing, unlocks, and codex systems.

02

Core Gameplay Loop

Enter, read, weaponize, clear, route

1

Room Entry

The player crosses a threshold, doors seal, the room modifier banner appears, and neutral fixtures pulse with a faint gold tell.

2

Threat Read

Enemy spawn pads ignite, cover and trap lanes become readable, and the player chooses whether to open aggressively or kite toward a fixture.

3

Combat Flow

Primary attacks, dash movement, parries, and the heat economy create openings. Consecrated traps are the run's force multiplier.

4

Room Clear

When the last wave falls, doors unseal, a flag or chest marker rises, rewards spill into the room, and the minimap exposes the next choice.

5

Meta Decision

The player picks one reward, optionally spends coins or keys, then commits to the next node type and district route.

Room Resolution Rules

  • Standard combat rooms spawn 1 to 2 waves and target a 45 to 70 second clear time.
  • Elite rooms guarantee one Rare-or-better reward and a larger Ash payout.
  • Treasury rooms are low threat but key-gated.
  • Boss rooms clear the district, award a seal, and return the player to the sanctuary strip.

Death and Restart

Coins, keys, temporary relics, devotions, and route progress are lost on death. Ash is banked for sanctuary upgrades. Victory applies a 1.5x Ash multiplier and unlocks higher challenge tiers.

Ash payout formula: (rooms x 3) + (elites x 8) + (bosses x 20) + no-hit bonuses

Doors lock on entry Room modifier every chamber Two-exit routing after most clears Sanctuary returns after bosses Meta bank on death or victory

Player Mechanics

Two vessels, one shared combat language

Dungeon_Character.png

Votive Warden

The heavier starter. Stronger guard economy, tighter melee cadence, and safer trap setup windows.

  • Starting weapon: Ash Blade
  • Starting rite: Ward Circle
  • Passive trait: Brass Lung (+15 max Heat before burnout)
  • Base stats: 7 Health, 110 Resolve, 100 Heat cap
Dungeon_Character_2.png

Red Courier

The skirmisher. Faster repositioning, stronger pull combos, and better ranged trap conversion lines.

  • Starting weapon: Hand Crossbow
  • Starting rite: Chain Pull
  • Passive trait: Grave Step (+20% dash distance)
  • Base stats: 6 Health, 100 Resolve, 100 Heat cap

Movement

Free 8-direction movement on a 16x16 collision grid. Keyboard and mouse or twin-stick supported. Base move speed targets 105 world units per second.

Dash

0.22 seconds of invulnerability, 25 Resolve cost, 1.6 second cooldown on repeated use through stamina gating instead of a hard timer.

Parry and Brace

8-frame parry window. Holding the input after the window converts it into a brace stance that reduces incoming damage by 60% while draining Resolve.

Shared Combat Verbs

Verb Base Input Function Tuning Target
Primary Attack Left mouse / right stick fire Weapon combo or projectile string 4 to 8 Heat generated per completed attack action
Consecrate Interact near fixture Converts a neutral trap or light source into an allied hazard 20 Heat, 0.35 second channel, 8 second active duration
Dash Space / dodge button Reposition, avoid telegraphs, cross flame lanes 25 Resolve cost
Parry / Brace Right mouse / left trigger Reflects projectiles, stuns melee, or soft-blocks when held 20 Resolve start cost, 15 Resolve per second while bracing
Rite Slot Q / shoulder button Supplemental active power such as Lantern Burst or Chain Pull 20 to 40 Heat depending on rite

Health

Persistent through the run. Standard start is 6 to 7 pips. Healing is scarce and mostly tied to sanctuaries, blessings, and flask pickups.

Resolve

Regenerating defensive stamina used by dash, parry, and brace. Starts refilling 0.75 seconds after last spend. Taking damage removes 15 Resolve immediately.

Censer Heat

Offensive ritual fuel generated by attacks and room control. Reaching 100 triggers Burnout, locking rites for 5 seconds and dropping all active consecrations.

Run Progression

Every 4 cleared rooms grants a Devotion pick from 3 options: max Health, max Resolve, Heat efficiency, trap damage, or economy.

Room and Level Design

One tileset, three districts, many tactical moods

District I

Ember Gate

Onboarding district focused on arrow lanes, boxes, small torch clusters, and readable enemy pairings.

  • Threat budget: 5 to 8
  • Enemy cap: 4 active
  • Fixture focus: arrows, torches, crates
District II

Ossuary Vaults

Dense skeleton formations, spike fields, locked rewards, and skull support units that force better lane control.

  • Threat budget: 8 to 12
  • Enemy cap: 6 active
  • Fixture focus: peaks, keys, mini chests
District III

Crimson Reliquary

Faster elites, flamethrower lanes, trap reversals, and vampire pressure built around mobility and timing.

  • Threat budget: 12 to 16
  • Enemy cap: 7 active
  • Fixture focus: flames, side torches, high-value chests

Room Types

Type Purpose Frequency Reward Pattern
Combat Standard kill room with one modifier and one fixture focus 8 per district Pick 1 of 2 upgrades or currency bundles
Hazard Lower enemy count but denser trap layouts Mixed into combat pool Higher Heat economy and trap-themed rewards
Elite One elite plus support enemies 1 per district Guaranteed Rare or Relic
Treasury Optional treasure room with key expenditure 0 to 1 per district Mini chest clusters or one large chest
Blessing Priory echo shrine with healing and rule-bending boons 1 per district path Heal, cleanse Burnout, or gain a district-long modifier
Reliquary Shop Unmanned altar-shop with coin expenditure 0 to 1 per district Buy flasks, keys, rerolls, or one curated relic
Boss Hand-authored district climax 1 per district District Seal, heal burst, boss relic choice

Procedural Generation Rules

  • Each district uses an 11-node route template: 8 standard rooms, 1 elite, 1 service node, 1 boss.
  • Room layouts pull from modular templates between 10x8 and 18x12 tiles with fixed door, trap, spawn, and cover sockets.
  • Each room guarantees at least one consecratable object and one safe opening path from the entry threshold.
  • Spawn pads stay at least 3 tiles away from the player entry tile to preserve readability.

Difficulty Scaling

  • Scaling favors enemy composition and room rules before raw health inflation.
  • Every district adds a new pressure layer: support enemies, key gates, or trap reversal behavior.
  • Elite rooms increase move complexity and telegraph density rather than simply doubling stats.
  • Bosses are hand-authored so the run always ends in escalating, memorable set pieces.

Enemies and AI

Small roster, sharp roles, remixable behavior

Rattlebone

Basic chaser | Skeleton 1

  • HP 45, fast approach, 0.55 second slash tell
  • Backpedals from active flames but commits through spike lanes
  • Used to teach trap baiting and parry timing early

Ossuary Warder

Blocker | Skeleton 2

  • HP 80, frontal guard reduces 70% damage
  • Shield bash displaces the player into hazard lanes
  • Designed to make flanks, pull effects, and spikes matter

Reliquary Skull

Turret and support | Skull

  • HP 35, hovering bolt caster with short hover drift
  • Overcharges nearby traps to increase fire rate or pulse speed
  • Ignores dormant spikes but is vulnerable to consecrated ones

Crimson Kin

Assassin | Vampire

  • HP 70, blink slash, retreat leap, short life-drain bite
  • Prefers dark corners and broken line-of-sight
  • Enters late runs as the mobility check enemy

Elite Variants

  • Bone Captain: Skeleton 2 commander with command aura and arrow volley triggers.
  • Bellringer Skull: Skull variant that periodically accelerates all room hazards for 4 seconds.
  • Blood Acolyte: Vampire elite that marks the player and gains life steal on marked hits.

Scaling Philosophy

  • Difficulty rises through enemy synergies, trap coordination, and less forgiving room modifiers.
  • Basic enemies gain new support patterns across districts rather than huge health inflation.
  • Late rooms mix blockers, skull amplifiers, and blink units to test route planning and target priority.

Burn

Applied by torches and flamethrowers. Deals ticking damage and increases Heat gain from hitting the target.

Fracture

Applied by parries, heavy blade hits, and spikes. Reduces poise and increases trap damage taken.

Marked

Applied by vampires and some relics. Marked enemies are easier to pull and take bonus damage from consecrated attacks.

Suppressed

Applied to skulls when hit by a consecrated arrow volley, temporarily shutting down their trap-overcharge aura.

06

Boss Design

Three fights that rewrite how the room behaves

District I Boss

Warden of the Gate

A hulking skeleton captain who treats the arena as a shield corridor. This fight teaches the player to reposition between cover, punish frontal defense through trap use, and respect lane telegraphs.

  • Phase 1: Shield walk, arrow lane calls, summon pairs of Rattlebones.
  • Phase 2: Shield shatters at 60% health. Gains charge rushes and manual trap reversals.
  • Unique rule: Arrow slits only become allied if consecrated after a successful parry.
  • Reward: Gate Seal, full heal burst, choice of 1 boss relic.
District II Boss

Choir of Cinders

Three linked skulls orbit a central pyre and sing different hazard patterns into the room. The fight is about target ordering, area denial, and using torches to expose vulnerability windows.

  • Phase 1: One skull fires lines, one summons spikes, one overcharges torches.
  • Phase 2: Shared health breaks at 50%; all three sweep the arena with staggered fire arcs.
  • Phase 3: Final skull enrages and mirrors the last player-used trap type.
  • Reward: Vault Seal, 2-key payout, guaranteed Profane-tier choice.
Final Boss

The Crimson Prior

The vampire architect of the corrupted reliquary. This fight evolves from a duelist encounter into a room-control war where the boss steals and reverses the player's best tools.

  • Phase 1: Blink slash, blood clones, short grab punish on greedy swings.
  • Phase 2: Summons skeleton and skull support while reversing one active trap every cycle.
  • Phase 3: Darkens the room, ignites all side torches, and gains life drain on marked targets.
  • Reward: Credits clear, Ascension unlock, victory Ash multiplier, codex completion update.

Progression System

Reset the run, keep the priory

Resets Every Run

  • Weapons and temporary weapon modifiers
  • Rite pickups and passive relics
  • Coins, keys, devotions, and route state
  • Current Health, Resolve, and Heat state

Persists Between Runs

  • Banked Ash currency
  • Unlocked vessels, weapons, and rites
  • Priest upgrade trees
  • Codex entries, challenge tiers, and achievements

Sister Calve

Hearthkeeper

Handles healing, flask unlocks, sanctuary recovery, and persistent survivability upgrades.

Archivist Nhal

Keeper of Ash

Unlocks codex entries, challenge seeds, lore, and modifier pools.

Father Rusk

Rite Smith

Unlocks weapons, rite variants, reroll charges, and the second vessel.

Meta Progression Philosophy

Permanent progression is horizontal-first. Players unlock more options, not runaway stat inflation. Fully upgraded sanctuary bonuses should cap around +20% survivability and +15% economy so mastery stays more important than grind.

HUD Layout

  • Top-left: Health pips and Resolve meter.
  • Top-center: room name, modifier, and boss phase labels.
  • Top-right: Heat, keys, coin, and Ash counters.
  • Bottom-center: weapon, rite, interact prompts, and cooldown state.
  • Bottom-left: minimap strip with route icons.

Feedback Rules

  • Heavy hits use 40ms hit-stop and low-amplitude camera shake.
  • Consecrated traps gain gold telegraphs, hostile traps stay crimson.
  • Parries play a clean bell-like impact and brief white flash.
  • All damage numbers are optional and off by default.

Menu Stack

Title, sanctuary, codex, upgrades, pause, run summary, and challenge selection. No deep sub-menu nesting.

Navigation

The provided arrow interface sprites become the selection cursor, minimap node indicators, and room-choice pointers.

Accessibility

Rebinds, flash reduction, screen shake slider, colorblind telegraphs, aim assist, auto-fire option, and subtitle scaling.

Art and Audio Direction

Gothic pixel ritualism with warm light against dead stone

Ember Gate

Charcoal, brass, ember orange, and warm torchlight for the introductory descent.

Ossuary Vaults

Bone ivory, green-grey stone, and cold chapel teal to emphasize skeletal pressure and key routes.

Crimson Reliquary

Wine red, soot black, and candle gold for the final district's corrupted liturgy.

Visual Style

Ashen Reliquary uses 16x16 pixel art at a 4x presentation scale. It should feel intimate, severe, and ceremonial rather than cartoonish. Light sources are emotional anchors. Rooms should read as stone, ash, brass, and sacramental fire.

Animation Priorities

  • Clear attack tells and reaction frames beat large frame counts.
  • Torches, candles, flames, arrows, and coins should always feel alive.
  • Enemy motion stays readable first and expressive second.

Combat Sound

Dry metal, bone crack, chain drag, torch roar, and bell-like parry impacts.

Ambient Sound

Low chapel air, braziers, distant chanting, rattling chains, and subtle room tone by district.

Music

Layered percussion, bowed drones, low organ, and choir fragments that intensify when rooms lock and bosses phase.

Monetization

Premium, clean, and expansion-friendly

Core Model

Premium release priced in the $14.99 to $19.99 range. No premium currency, no energy systems, no paid power, and no content pacing tuned around retention extraction.

Post-Launch

Suitable post-launch add-ons are an OST, artbook, or a later challenge-focused expansion. Core balance, characters, and win conditions remain fully included in the base purchase.

Technical Design

Small-team friendly architecture in Godot 4.x

Engine

Godot 4.x for fast 2D iteration, tilemap tooling, data-driven scenes, and strong scriptability for a small indie team.

Camera

Orthographic top-down camera locked to the current room with subtle screen shake and boss-specific framing zooms.

Mode

Single-player only. No online systems, no co-op, and no network-dependent features in scope.

Runtime Systems

  • Encounter Director: builds room waves from a district threat budget.
  • Room State Controller: handles door lock, reward state, room modifiers, and exit reveal.
  • Trap Controller: tracks dormant, primed, consecrated, and hostile reversal states.
  • Loot Resolver: rolls reward tiers, shop stock, and devotion picks.
  • Enemy Brain: lightweight state machine per role with shared perception hooks.

Performance Targets

  • 60 FPS on low-spec PC with 320x180 internal render scale.
  • Object pooling for projectiles, hit VFX, and recurring trap pulses.
  • Max 7 active enemies outside boss encounters.
  • No runtime navmesh rebuilds; grid-aware steering is enough.

Replayability and Retention

Runs stay fresh through combinations, not bloat

Randomization

Room templates, modifiers, reward offers, district branch choices, and elite placements all shift from seed to seed.

Build Diversity

Two vessels, four weapons, four passive families, three districts, and trap-state play create many workable builds without overloading art needs.

Daily Run

Fixed seed, one curated mutator package, leaderboard-compatible score rules, and no meta upgrades beyond unlock pool access.

Weekly Challenge

Boss rush, one-hit kills for the player, or "all traps hostile until parried" variants for highly engaged players.

Achievements

  • Clear a district without taking trap damage.
  • Kill the Warden using only allied arrow slits.
  • Beat Choir of Cinders without extinguishing a torch.
  • Clear the game with each vessel.
  • Finish a run while triggering Burnout five times or less.

Retention Philosophy

Retention comes from mastery, unlock discovery, and challenge modes. The game should invite "one more run" through strong routing decisions and build experimentation, not through artificial daily chores.

Example Run

A full victory path through the Reliquary

Sanctuary Start

Pick the Red Courier

The player enters the run with Hand Crossbow, Chain Pull, 6 Health, and a small Ash investment that unlocks Grey Flasks in shops.

Ember Gate

Learn arrow lanes

The first rooms teach kiting through boxes, consecrating torches, and using Chain Pull to drag Rattlebones into allied arrows.

Elite Room

Fight a Bone Captain

The player parries a shield bash, flips an arrow wall, and earns the Rare relic Brass Tally, boosting coin drops and reroll odds.

Boss I

Defeat the Warden of the Gate

By saving Heat for consecrations after parries, the player wins a clean first boss and chooses Furnace Link, a relic that spreads Burn from torches.

Sanctuary Return

Buy safety and route forward

Sister Calve restores 2 Health. Father Rusk offers Lantern Burst as the new active rite. The run pivots into burn-heavy control play.

Ossuary Vaults

Shift into control

Skulls begin overcharging peaks. The player uses Chain Pull to pin Warders onto spikes, then uses Lantern Burst to expose skull support safely.

Treasury Detour

Spend two keys on a large chest

A great chest yields Profane relic Pyre Tithe: +25% torch damage, but Burnout lasts one extra second. The build becomes riskier and much stronger.

Boss II

Break the Choir of Cinders

The player consecrates side torches to expose the support skull first, survives the triple flame sweep, and claims the Vault Seal plus a full heal.

Crimson Reliquary

Late-run pressure test

Vampires blink across flame lanes, hostile reversals force disciplined Heat spending, and one bad room nearly triggers a fatal Burnout chain.

Final Boss

Win by stealing the room back

The Crimson Prior reverses side torches in phase two, but the player saves Lantern Burst to interrupt a drain attempt, then uses two consecrated flamethrowers to secure the kill.

Victory

Cash out and unlock Ascension

The player banks the victory Ash multiplier, unlocks the first Ascension modifier, and returns to the sanctuary with new codex pages and a second vessel unlock path nearly complete.
